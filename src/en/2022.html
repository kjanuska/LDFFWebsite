Kernagis

Kernagis is the first documentary about the maestro Vytautas Kernagis, one of the most famous Lithuanian artists of all time. This film is an irreplaceable look into his processes, thoughts, and creative being. Director Andrius Lekavičius invites viewers to enter the world of V. Kernagis, which is being opened for the first time thanks to the intimate and never-before-seen family archive.

Gentle Warriors

Three young girls decide to volunteer for military service after the conscription for men is reintroduced in Lithuania as a response to possible threats from neighboring Russia. The girls live and train among 600 men at an isolated military base for 9 months. How will this experience shift their physical and emotional well beings, or their mentality when they find that the true war is within themselves?

Moncys. Samogitian de Paris

Who was Antanas Mončys? A Lithuanian. A Samogitian. An emigrant. A Parisian. A son, father, husband, brother, sculptor. He traveled from a small Liothuanian town to Paris, from the shores of the Baltic sea to the thundering waves of the Atlantic, all the while carrying his Lithuanian heritage with him. He was an artist who merged an archaic Lithuanian worldview with modern Western European art to create an artform in which we can see ourselves.

Back from New York

Back from New York is a film about two unique artists and close friends, photographer Arūnas Kulikauskas and painter Eugenijus Varkulevičius-Varkalis, whose lives were greatly influenced by emigration to the bustling and enchanting New York City during the Soviet times. After many years abroad, they return back to their homeland to start again. Follow their story as it delves into not only their artistic discoveries in NYC, but also about their true joys of life.

Blue/ Red /Deport

37-year-old Afghan filmmaker Talib Shah Hossaini finds himself on the verge of losing hope after living one year in Moria, the biggest refugee camp in Europe. Instead of giving up, however, he makes a fictional film in which he explores the camp’s complex reality and indefinite future as refugees. Director Lina Luzyte observes Talib Shah, using the meta film as a magnifying glass to blow-up reality.

Jump

In 1970, Lithuanian sailor Simas Kudirka made a desperate leap for freedom from his Soviet vessel to a US Coast Guard cutter asking for asylum. The Americans sent him back inciting one of the Cold War's most complex and suspenseful political blunders before a stunning twist of fate.
